Grape Sunburn Spreads as Climate Change Reaches New Wine Regions

  • Direct sun exposure can push berry surface temperatures 22-27°F above ambient air, meaning a 95°F day creates surfaces exceeding 122°F on sun-facing sides.
  • Traditional mid-summer leaf removal now increases sunburn risk during heat waves; early-season defoliation allows berries to build photoprotective compounds, reducing damage.
  • Kaolin clay particle film reduces canopy temperatures by up to 9°C; some producers have eliminated summer leaf removal on white varieties entirely.
  • Sunburned grapes produce cooked and oxidized flavors that worsen with aging, and producers increasingly rely on harvest sorting as a final defense.

Organic Sauvignon Blanc demands careful vineyard work to overcome disease sensitivity

  • Sauvignon Blanc is complex to grow organically because it's vulnerable to powdery and downy mildew, requiring frequent treatments.
  • Copper-based fungicides, the primary permitted organic option against mildew, can damage Sauvignon Blanc's sensitive flowers.
  • Biodynamic farming builds living soil that helps vines overcome disease and climate stress, according to practitioners in France and Austria.
  • Years of organic farming can increase Sauvignon Blanc's natural disease resilience, as demonstrated by growers in southern France.
  • Organic Sauvignon Blancs range from mineral and grassy styles to tropical fruit-forward expressions depending on terroir and winemaking methods.
