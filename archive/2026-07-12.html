France’s Heatwave Cuts Wine Harvest as Growers Brace for Smaller Vintage

  • France's heatwave is reducing wine harvests across Champagne, Bordeaux, and Burgundy, with Champagne expecting ~10% production decline and Bordeaux potentially down 20% or more.
  • Extreme heat accelerates grape ripening, risking higher alcohol levels, lower acidity, and imbalanced wines despite appearing ripe.
  • Champagne may harvest around August 15, potentially the earliest on record; Burgundy around August 20; Bordeaux Crémant as early as August 1.
  • Smaller global supply unlikely to create widespread shortages due to weak demand and excess inventory, though premium appellations and white wines face tighter supplies.
  • Burgundy's Premier Cru and Grand Cru whites remain undersupplied, making production declines more impactful there than in village-tier reds.
