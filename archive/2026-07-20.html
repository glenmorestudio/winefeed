Paul Boutinot steps back from wine distributor after 33-year run

  • Paul Boutinot sold his eponymous wine distributor to its management team in 2013, ending his 33-year ownership since founding it in Stockport in 1980.

US buyers lift fine wine market to price stability in H1 2026

  • US fine wine buyers increased their share to 26.9% of purchase value in Q2 2026, up from 20.7% average in 2025, according to Liv-ex data.
  • American buyers paid an average 1.1% premium above market price in Q2, compared to 0.03% in Q1, supporting overall price stability across fine wine indices.
  • Asian buyers retreated during H1 as shipping costs surged up to 60% due to the Iran War, while private collectors now drive the market as merchants remain risk-averse.
  • Liv-ex forecasts price stability will likely continue if US buying holds through H2 and purchase prices remain at or above market levels.
  • Top-line stability masks selective demand; trade volumes remain depressed despite index gains, creating what Liv-ex calls quiet opportunity for disciplined buyers.

Australian wine harvest hits 25-year low as prices fall further

  • Australia's 2026 crush dropped to 1.27 million tonnes, down 19% from 2025 and 25% below the 10-year average, according to Wine Australia.
  • Vintage value fell 26% to A$837 million despite the smaller harvest, with grape prices falling 6% year-on-year on average.
  • Red varieties accounted for 80% of the production decline; white grapes now make up the majority of the crush for only the second time in 12 years.
  • Wine Australia's Peter Bailey and Sandy Hathaway attributed the collapse to weak global demand and extreme weather including late frosts, heatwaves and flooding.
  • Four consecutive vintages have now fallen below the long-term average, signalling what Bailey called an underlying reset in grapes required by winemakers.

Rosé's boom risks collapse if producers chase excessive leanness

  • Patrick Schmitt MW notes rosé is one of few growth categories in struggling wine markets, loved across age groups from Jeremy Clarkson to Kylie Jenner.
  • Rosé's appeal rests on balance: dry and mouthwatering with red-berry and stone-fruit flavours, crisp finish, and weight on mid-palate without sweetness or heavy tannins.
  • Schmitt detected a stylistic drift toward excessive leanness in The Global Rosé Masters blind tasting, with many rosés tasting herbaceous, hard, or thin rather than soft.
  • He warns the category risks the fate of 1990s Chardonnay, which lost consumers when a pendulum swing toward skinny, cool-climate styles overcorrected from oaky excess.
  • To preserve rosé's success, producers should retain ripe fruit, moderate alcohol, and soft texture in everyday offerings; experimentation belongs at the premium end only.

Texas A&M sends grape seeds to space station to study radiation effects

  • Hundreds of Texas grape seeds launched to the International Space Station as part of a six-month Texas A&M University experiment studying cosmic radiation impact on plant genetics.
  • Aerospace engineering students Coby Arnold and Arvind Subramanyam designed a protective carrier allowing seeds to survive radiation exposure while triggering genetic changes, working with viticulture specialist Justin Scheiner.

Grape sunburn spreads beyond hot regions as heat waves intensify

  • Sunburn occurs when berry surface temperatures exceed 104-109°F under high light, causing chlorophyll and aromatic compounds to degrade within minutes.
  • Chris Tanghe, MS and education director at GuildSomm, identifies sunburned fruit by rubber, asphalt, and creosote notes alongside flattened fruit and oxidized bitterness.
  • Joanna Gambetta, Ph.D., wine scientist at University of Newcastle, Australia, found that early defoliation at flowering builds photoprotective compounds, while defoliation at veraison increases damage significantly.
  • Producers including Tyrrell's in Hunter Valley and Chêne Bleu in Ventoux now limit or eliminate midsummer leaf removal and apply kaolin clay reflective film to cool canopies by up to 9°C.
  • Winemakers including Steve Matthiasson at Matthiasson Wines in Napa Valley now prioritize canopy cover and vineyard design over sun exposure, reversing decades of defoliation orthodoxy.

Heat-Tolerant Mourvèdre Struggles as Southern France Temperatures Soar

  • Mourvèdre, bred for heat in Spain's Levante and long dominant in coastal Bandol, France, is losing balance as harvests accelerate and acidity drops under record temperatures.
  • Even heat-adapted vines require moderation: a three-year drought in Jumilla from 2022 to 2024 killed old bush vines, forcing growers to consider irrigation despite terroir concerns.
  • Bandol and Jumilla producers are experimenting with canopy techniques, raised gobelet training, three-dimensional shade, cover crops, to slow ripening and retain soil moisture.
  • Cooler Bandol sites traditionally reserved for rosé are now being planted for red wine, risking a shift away from the region's current 75 percent rosé production.
  • Viticulture professor Alain Deloire warns dry farming is unsustainable in Provence, claiming irrigation is now compulsory for most vineyards despite growers' fears of losing terroir typicity.
