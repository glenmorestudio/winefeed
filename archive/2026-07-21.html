Bird in Hand expands cool-climate operation from Adelaide Hills to Tasmania

  • Bird in Hand, founded by Andrew and Susie Nugent in Adelaide Hills in 1997, is opening its first operation outside South Australia in Tasmania's Tamar Valley with a 10-hectare vineyard.
  • UK premium wine exports above £7.50 per litre are up 15% while cheap commodity wine declines, creating opportunity for Bird in Hand's terroir-focused positioning.

Bordeaux vineyard land values collapse as sales fall below three million hectolitres

  • Bordeaux shipments fell to 2.98 million hectolitres in the 12 months to March 2026, down 12% year-on-year and below three million for the first time in recorded history.
  • Vineyard land prices in Médoc have fallen 81.8% since 2018 to €10,000 per hectare; Fronsac down 50% and Bordeaux Rouge down 60.6% over the same period.
  • Prestigious appellations proved more resilient: Pauillac land fell 22.7% to €1.7m per hectare, while Margaux dropped 27.3% to €800,000 per hectare.
  • Nearly 30,000 hectares of vines were uprooted between 2023 and 2026, with two-thirds receiving government compensation of €4,000 or €6,000 per hectare.
  • Producers including Xavier Buffo at Château de la Rivière in Fronsac are consolidating production on best terroirs while selling marginal land to refocus operations.

Taylor's Port cuts bottle weight 24% while keeping iconic design intact

  • Taylor's Port, a major fortified-wine producer, reduced its fine-wine bottle from 580g to 440g starting July 21, saving 350 tonnes of glass and 210 tonnes of CO2 yearly.
  • CEO Adrian Bridge deliberately downplayed the change, arguing consumers should not notice the lighter bottle on shelf and that genuine sustainability matters more than marketing claims.
  • The debossed lettering on the bottle's shoulder, an iconic feature from the early 1990s, was preserved despite being a weak point that prevented reaching 420g target weight.
  • Taylor's also switched to a fully biodegradable fibre-based stopper and relocated the mold-cavity mark from base to side to allow thinner glass at the punt.
  • The company uses recycled Euro-green glass at 85% content, produced by Spanish manufacturer Vidrala in Portugal, and plans to extend the lightweighting to sister brand Croft and other products.

Texas A&M sends grape seeds to space station to breed radiation-resistant vines

  • Hundreds of grape seeds from Texas will orbit the International Space Station for six months as part of a Texas A&M University experiment measuring cosmic radiation's effect on plant DNA.
  • Aerospace engineering students Coby Arnold and Arvind Subramanyam designed a protective carrier that shields seeds from lethal radiation while allowing enough exposure to trigger genetic mutations.

Grape sunburn spreads as heat waves reach cooler regions; producers shift vineyard practices

  • Sunburn in grapes is a stress response triggered when berry surface temperatures exceed 104-109°F under high light, causing chlorophyll and polyphenols to degrade and producing asphalt, rubber, and creosote aromas.
  • Direct sun exposure can push berry surface temperatures 22-27°F above ambient air, meaning a 95°F day creates 122°F on sun-facing fruit; damage occurs within minutes at extreme temperatures.
  • Chêne Bleu in Ventoux and Tyrrell's in Hunter Valley have abandoned mid-summer defoliation practices used for decades, instead protecting fruit with retained canopy or kaolin clay reflective films.
  • Defoliation timing matters critically: early leaf removal allows berries to build photoprotective compounds, while defoliation just before heat waves dramatically increases sunburn risk.
  • As heat waves arrive earlier and reach traditionally cooler regions, vineyard canopy management and sorting are becoming primary defenses against sunburned grapes in the finished wine.
