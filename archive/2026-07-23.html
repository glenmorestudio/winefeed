Bordeaux vineyard land values collapse as sales sink below three million hectolitres

  • Bordeaux shipments fell to 2.98 million hectolitres in the 12 months to March 2026, down 12% year-on-year and below the three million hectolitre threshold for the first time in modern history.
  • Vineyard land prices fell steeply across appellations: Pauillac down 22.7% to €1.7m per hectare since 2018, Margaux down 27.3% to €800,000, and Saint-Émilion down 25.9% to €200,000.

  • Nearly 30,000 hectares of Bordeaux vines were uprooted between 2023 and 2026, with two-thirds funded through government compensation schemes paying €4,000 to €6,000 per hectare.
  • Producers including Xavier Buffo at Château de la Rivière in Fronsac are consolidating by removing weaker vineyards and acquiring land in stronger locations to improve profitability.

  • M.A.Silva, a Portuguese cork producer, integrated an AI system called Bionic Eye into quality control for its Nobeltech closure line.

Costco recalls desert willow plants carrying grape-killing pest eggs

  • Costco customers who bought desert willow plants between 24 June and 3 July should inspect them for glassy-winged sharpshooter eggs, an invasive leafhopper native to the southeastern US and northeastern Mexico.
  • The insect transmits Xylella fastidiosa, the bacterium causing Pierce's disease, a fatal infection that blocks water flow in grapevines, causing leaf discoloration, shriveled grapes, and vine death.
  • Pierce's disease has no cure; infected vines must be removed and destroyed immediately to prevent spread, according to the USDA.
  • This is the second Costco plant recall in 2026 involving the pest; California's $73 billion wine industry faces particular vulnerability to the disease.
  • Customers with planted trees should isolate them and contact their county agricultural commissioner; disposed plants must be double-bagged and placed in household rubbish, never compost.

Grape sunburn shifts vineyard practices as heat waves intensify globally

  • Sunburn in grapes is a stress response to excess light and heat that damages chlorophyll, polyphenols, and aromatic compounds, producing sensory notes of cooked fruit, asphalt, and creosote in finished wine.
  • Defoliation timing now critical: leaf removal at veraison increases sunburn risk, while removal at flowering allows berries to build photoprotective compounds, reversing decades of standard practice in warm regions.
  • Producers including Tyrrell's in Australia's Hunter Valley and Chêne Bleu in Provence's Ventoux have abandoned summer defoliation, adopted kaolin clay sprays to cool canopy by up to nine degrees Celsius, and limited fruit exposure.
  • Winemakers including Steve Matthiasson of Matthiasson Wines in Napa Valley now design vineyards to provide canopy shade and sort sunburned fruit at harvest, treating direct sun as a liability rather than an asset for ripeness.
