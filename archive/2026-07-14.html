Grape sunburn damage spreads as heat waves reach cooler wine regions

  • Sunburned grapes show sensory markers: flattened fruit, rubber, asphalt, creosote notes, plus cooked flavors, brown rims, high alcohol, and flabby acid.
  • Direct sun pushes berry surface temps 22-27°F above air temperature; Chardonnay shows damage within five minutes above 104-109°F under high light.
  • Leaf removal timing is critical: defoliation at flowering builds photoprotective compounds; defoliation at veraison or before heat waves increases sunburn risk significantly.
  • Vintage strategy has shifted: Chêne Bleu in Ventoux eliminated summer leaf removal after 2023 heat wave; Tyrrell's in Hunter Valley relies on kaolin since 2014.

Organic Sauvignon Blanc challenges growers but rewards biodynamic farming

  • Sauvignon Blanc is difficult to grow organically because it's highly susceptible to powdery and downy mildew, requiring frequent fungicide treatment.
